General Tips

  • Write clearly and respectfully and double check your spelling - especially names!
  • Tailor your letter to your own representative and senators. For example, if one is already a leader on reform, encourage the others to follow suit.
  • Keep it short! You’ll have the best chance of getting published if you stay under 200 words.
  • If possible, cite a recent story from the publication.
  • Find your voice! Use these talking points as a guide, but write in your own words.

Talking Points

  1. "Big money pouring into our elections and corrupting the political process. It’s a huge problem that distorts policy and drowns out the needs of the American people." You could support your case by citing a related local event, local news story, or statistics. See examples.
    • Expensive campaigns force members of Congress to spend up to 70% of their time raising cash from special interests, instead of doing their job for the American people.
    • Last year, less than one out of every 50 Americans gave money to candidates for Congress. Among those who did make contributions, the 100 biggest donors gave as much as the smallest 4.75 million donors.
    • Most SuperPAC money last year came from just 100 people. The results? In the latest budget, President Obama and Congress changed the law to put us back on the hook for risky Wall Street derivative trading.
    • With current campaign statutes, no candidate can turn down huge money and hope to take (or keep) a seat in Congress. Only through fundamental campaign finance reform can this institutionalized corruption be stopped
  2. "I’m tired of corruption and everyone I know is demanding change." More.
    • The deck is stacked against us ordinary Americans; only when fundamental reform reduces the corrupting influence of huge money in elections can Congress people return to their calling: representing the people of this great nation.
    • One recent poll showed that 96% of Americans think it is critical that we “reduce the influence of money in politics.” I couldn't agree more, and everyone I talk to feels the same.
  3. Demand support for a specific bill or thank your legislator for their support. "My representatives in Congress should co-sponsor [a specific bill]." Find out if your representative or senators support reform here.
    • Representative ___________ can co-sponsor the Government By The People Act (H.R.20), a strong reform bill calling for citizen-funded elections that has already been introduced in Congress.
    • Senator(s) ___________ can co-sponsor the Fair Elections Now Act (S.1538), a strong reform bill calling for citizen-funded elections that has already been introduced in Congress.
    • Rep./Sen(s). ___________ has signed onto H.R.20 / S.1358, a strong reform bill calling for citizen-funded elections before Congress now, which is great news. Rep./Sen(s). ___________ should follow suit and lead on fundamental reform.
